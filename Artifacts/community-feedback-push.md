# CLI — Dev Hangout Community Feedback Push

## Objective

Get 10+ developers from the dev hangout community to deploy a container on CLI and return structured feedback within 7 days of early access opening.

## Approach

### Step 1: Seed Interest (Day 1)
Post the primary hook in the dev hangout chat (see dev-hangout-promo-messaging.md). Keep it conversational. Don't announce — contribute. Answer questions honestly. Understate where things are rough.

### Step 2: Direct Outreach (Day 1-2)
DM the highest-interest developers (Moose + anyone who engaged with the initial post) with a personal invite. Give them early access before the general opening. Make them feel like insiders.

Template:
"Hey — saw your interest in CLI. We're opening early access to a small group this week. You'd be deploying a container (Ollama, LLM endpoint, whatever you want) and telling us what breaks. No expectations of polish — we need real usage. Want in?"

### Step 3: Deploy and Collect (Day 2-5)
Once a developer deploys, collect feedback through a lightweight structure:

1. What did you deploy?
2. How long did it take from click to running?
3. What confused you?
4. What was missing?
5. Would you use this for real work? What would need to change?

Can be collected via DM, a Telegram form, or a simple Google Form. Keep friction near zero.

### Step 4: Close the Loop (Day 5-7)
Send each tester a personal summary of what changed based on their feedback. Even if it's small — "you said the deploy button was unclear, we added a progress indicator." This builds loyalty and makes them feel invested in the product.

```
3. Most confusing part:
4. What was missing:
5. Would you use this daily? (y/n/maybe)
6. One thing that would make you love it:
```

## Success Criteria

- 10+ developers deploy at least one container
- 8+ return completed feedback
- 3+ identify a bug or UX issue we hadn't caught
- 2+ ask to continue testing after the first round

## What Not To Do

- Don't post in multiple channels simultaneously — start with one community, own it
- Don't overpromise features that aren't built
- Don't ask for testimonials — ask for honesty
- Don't ghost testers after they deploy — follow up within 24h
